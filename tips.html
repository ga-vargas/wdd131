Plan Your Trip

Learn the best tips to travel through Argentina safely and comfortably.

Language

In Argentina, the official language is Spanish. Although it is more common to find people who speak English in big cities, knowing a bit of basic Spanish is recommended to enhance your travel experience, especially if you plan to visit more rural areas of the country.

Exchange Rates

The exchange rate varies widely in Argentina. Understanding the difference between the official rate and the 'blue dollar' can help you manage your budget better. Although it is not a 'legal' practice, it is very common to find people or stores where you can exchange dollars for Argentine pesos at a better rate. Many places also accept payments in dollars, and credit cards are widely accepted in stores throughout the country.

Safety Precautions

Argentina is one of the safest countries in Latin America, with crime statistics much lower than those of other countries in the region, such as Mexico, Brazil, or Colombia, making it largely safe for tourists. However, as in many large cities around the world, it’s important to take precautions against pickpocketing, luggage theft, and belongings left in cars, among other types of crime. It’s advisable to avoid areas that are not recommended for tourists, such as villas (low-income neighborhoods), or going to less-visited places at night.

Cultural Etiquette

Meal times in Argentina differ from those in many other countries. Lunch is typically enjoyed around 1 p.m., while dinner is often much later, between 8 and 10 p.m., or even later, especially on weekends. Embrace the opportunity to enjoy the local customs, as mealtimes are often leisurely and social. Many restaurants offer merienda (a traditional afternoon snack) around 5 p.m., where locals gather to enjoy coffee and pastries. Dining in Argentina is more than just a meal; it’s a time to connect with friends and family over good food.
